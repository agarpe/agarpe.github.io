Alicia Garrido Peña

I am a predoctoral researcher in the Biological Neurocomputation Group, finalizing my thesis in the field of Computational Neuroscience. I hold a degree in Computer Engineering from UGR and have completed master's degrees in Computer Engineering and R&D in ICTs from UAM.

My thesis takes a hybrid approach, conducting electrophysiological recordings in invertebrates (Lymnaea stagnalis snails), simulating conductance-based computational neuronal models, and designing closed-loop protocols for real-time activity-dependent stimulation. The focus of my thesis has been the use of these techniques to study sequences in Central Pattern Generators (CPGs) and the effect of near-infrared laser, a novel non-invasive neurostimulation technique.

My other interests include computer science, artificial intelligence, open and inclusive science, and free software.
